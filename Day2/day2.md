# Day 2: What Is Prompt Engineering? 🧠

**Date:** Day 2 of the ABTalks 60 Days of AI Coding Challenge  
**Topic:** Prompt Engineering — Lazy vs. Engineered Prompts

---

## 🎯 What I Learned Today

Prompt Engineering is the art of crafting precise, contextual instructions to get the best possible output from AI models like Claude or ChatGPT.

**Key insight:** The quality of your output is directly proportional to the quality of your input prompt.

---

## 📊 Before vs. After: Prompt Comparison

### ❌ Lazy Prompt
```
Create an image explaining Prompt Engineering.
```

**What AI gave me:** A vague, generic response. The AI had to guess what "explaining" meant, who the audience was, what format, what level of detail, and what style.

**Output quality:** Generic, unmemorable, required 3+ follow-ups to get usable content.

---

### ✅ Engineered Prompt
```
You are a visual educator creating content for beginners.

Create a detailed explanation of Prompt Engineering using this structure:
1. Define Prompt Engineering in one simple sentence (no jargon)
2. Show a BEFORE example (a bad, vague prompt) and AFTER example (a clear, structured prompt) for explaining quantum physics to a 10-year-old
3. List the 5 key elements of a great prompt: Role, Context, Task, Format, Constraints
4. End with one actionable tip the reader can use TODAY

Tone: Friendly, encouraging, practical. Audience: Complete beginners with no AI experience.
```

**What AI gave me:** A structured, complete, usable piece of content that required zero follow-ups.

**Output quality:** Clear, actionable, audience-appropriate — exactly what I needed on the first try.

---

## 🔑 Key Learnings

1. **Be specific about role** — Tell the AI who it is ("You are a...") before asking it anything
2. **Give context** — Who is the audience? What's the purpose?
3. **Define the format** — Numbered lists, paragraphs, tables? Say it explicitly
4. **Set constraints** — Tone, length, complexity level
5. **Iterate intentionally** — Each revision should change one variable at a time

---

## 💡 My Takeaway

The difference between a lazy prompt and an engineered prompt is like the difference between telling a chef "make something" vs. "make a 20-minute vegetarian pasta for 2 people, medium spice, no nuts."

**Same cook, completely different results — just because of how you asked.**
